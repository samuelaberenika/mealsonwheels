Our mission

Fighting hunger in Lagos,
one meal at a time

Lagos State, home to over 25 million residents, faces a food security crisis. An estimated 2 in every 10 people lack access to a nutritious meal each day — a burden deepened by widespread poverty and the lasting impact of the COVID-19 pandemic.

The Meals on Wheels programme, run by the Saint Vincent de Paul Society (SSVP), mobilises resources to provide daily meals to orphans, the sick and elderly, persons with disabilities, and the unemployed — ensuring no one goes to bed hungry.

The process

From registration to first meal

Every beneficiary goes through a structured three-stage process to ensure assistance reaches those who need it most.

Step 01

Registration

An authorised SSVP member fills in the beneficiary registration form online or in person, capturing personal details, eligibility criteria, and preferred meal type.

Step 02

Review & Approval

Society members review the submission in a scheduled meeting. The President and Secretary verify eligibility, confirm meal category, and set the duration of assistance.

Step 03

Confirmation & Delivery

Once approved, the beneficiary is notified with their start date, meal schedule, and assigned logistics partner. Deliveries begin as confirmed.

What we provide

Three categories of support

Each beneficiary receives assistance tailored to their specific circumstances.

Cooked Food

Prepared meals delivered to beneficiaries who are unable to cook — due to physical limitations, lack of facilities, or illness.

Once or twice daily · Up to 2 months per cycle

Raw Ingredients

Food supplies for beneficiaries who can cook but lack access to ingredients. Provided weekly or bi-weekly to supplement their existing food supply.

Weekly or bi-weekly · Up to 2 months per cycle

Combination

Both cooked meals and raw ingredients, reserved for special cases such as pregnancy, large families, or specific dietary requirements.

Custom schedule · Approved case-by-case

Beneficiary eligibility

Who we help

Only SSVP members may register beneficiaries. Applicants must meet one or more of the following criteria.

Job loss or no income

Individuals who have recently lost their job or have no sustainable means of income and cannot provide food for themselves or their families.

No support system

People who lack a support network — including orphans and children from families with no means of providing a minimum food ration.

Homeless, sick, or elderly

Homeless individuals with no food access, and sick or elderly persons with no or limited support from family members.

Unable to self-feed

Anyone who is unable to feed themselves and requires daily assistance with food due to physical disability or other severe circumstances.

Our network

Partners who make it possible

The Meals on Wheels service depends on a network of trusted partners committed to quality, reliability, and compassion.

Food Vendors

Cooked meal suppliers

  • Reasonable and affordable pricing
  • High standards of food hygiene
  • Weekly or monthly payment schedule
  • Capacity to supply for 5+ days during payment delays

Grocery Stores

Raw ingredient suppliers

  • Credit facility for minimum 1 week during delays
  • Fresh, high-quality foodstuffs
  • Reliable stock of staple ingredients
  • Flexible billing arrangements

Logistics Partners

Delivery & transport

  • Timely and safe meal delivery
  • Professional, courteous service
  • Internal or contracted riders
  • Reliable availability for daily runs
